**What are the key phases of the change management process within a project management office (PMO)? Select all that apply.** 

Implementation and execution—The PMO ignores existing project teams and unilaterally implements changes to expedite the Transformation.

**Awareness and communication – the PMO collaborates with leadership to communicate the need for Transformation, its benefits, and expected outcomes to ensure stakeholder understanding and support.**
Correct
That’s correct. Awareness and communication is the initial phase of the change management process within a PMO. It involves creating awareness about the need for change and effectively communicating its benefits and expected outcomes to all stakeholders. 

**Planning and preparation—The PMO develops a comprehensive change plan, defining the scope of the transformation, assessing project impacts, allocating resources, and creating risk mitigation strategies.**
Correct
That’s correct. Planning and preparation are crucial for laying a solid foundation for the change initiative. The PMO develops a comprehensive change management plan that defines the scope of the transformation, assesses its impact on current projects and resources, allocates necessary resources, and devises risk mitigation strategies. 

**Sustainability and reinforcement—The PMO embeds new processes through ongoing training, performance monitoring, and addressing resistance to reinforce benefits and ensure long-term success.**
Correct
That’s correct. This phase focuses on ensuring the changes are maintained over the long term. The PMO supports this by providing ongoing training, monitoring performance, and addressing any resistance that arises after implementation.
