**Strength 1:**
High employee engagement and ownership

**Strength 3:**

Slower decision-making and implementation

**Weakness 2:**
Risk of lack of strategic alignment

**Weakness 3:**
Potential fragmentation of initiatives across teams

---

**Strength 1:**
Balances strategic alignment with employee engagement

**Strength 2:**
Combines leadership direction with operational insight

**Strength 3:**
More resilient and adaptable during complex transformations

**Weakness 1:**
Requires strong coordination and governance

**Weakness 2:**
Can be more complex to manage

## **Step 2: Recommending the best approach**

### **Recommended Approach:**

**Hybrid approach**

---

### **Explain your selection**

The **hybrid approach** is the most suitable for TechInnovate because the company is undergoing a **complex digital transformation** that requires both:

* **strong strategic direction** (to align technology, processes, and business goals)
* **high employee engagement** (to ensure adoption, innovation, and sustainability)

A purely top-down model would risk resistance and low engagement, while a purely bottom-up model could lack speed and strategic coherence. The hybrid model combines the strengths of both.

---

### **Factors**

#### **Leadership involvement**

The hybrid model ensures that leadership defines the vision, priorities, and transformation roadmap, maintaining strong alignment with strategic objectives while providing clear direction across the organization.

#### **Employee engagement**

Employees are actively involved in shaping and improving implementation, increasing ownership, reducing resistance, and leveraging frontline knowledge to improve execution quality.

#### **Flexibility and adaptability**

The hybrid approach allows continuous feedback and adjustment, making it more resilient in dynamic environments where technologies, requirements, and priorities evolve rapidly.

**Question 1**
In this activity, you were tasked with recommending a change management model. As you reflect on the factors that are most important for choosing a change management approach, consider how these factors influenced your decision and explain why these considerations are crucial to managing change successfully.

The most important factors were employee engagement, leadership alignment, and the need to preserve innovation during transformation. These factors matter because each change model succeeds or fails based on how well the organization balances direction and adoption. A top-down approach gives clarity, speed, and strong leadership sponsorship, which is essential in a large digital transformation. However, on its own it can create resistance if employees feel that change is imposed on them. A bottom-up approach increases ownership, engagement, and practical innovation because employees closest to the work often identify realistic solutions, but it may lack consistency or strategic coordination. For TechInnovate, these considerations point toward a hybrid approach as the strongest option, because it combines strategic guidance from leadership with active involvement from employees. These considerations are crucial because successful change is not only about deciding what must change, but also about ensuring that people understand it, support it, and can implement it effectively. Leadership alignment keeps the transformation connected to business goals, employee engagement improves buy-in and reduces resistance, and innovation ensures the organization does not simply comply with change but uses it to improve how it works. In other words, change is most successful when it is both well-directed and well-adopted.
